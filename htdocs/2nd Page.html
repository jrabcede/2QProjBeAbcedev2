Introduction to Aquascaping

Aquascaping Trivia

  • Aquascaping started in the 1930's.

  • Aquascaping started when a scientist tried to prove that you can make a self-sustaining environment in an aquarium

  • A person succesfully aquascaped a 160,000 litre aquarium

Aquascaping Tips

  1. Aquascapers usually have the highest point of the scape at the back while the lowest point is at the middle.

  2. With the combination of plants, rocks, wood, and substrate you can create pieces that resemble above-land scenes.

  3. You can make a "tree" with wood and moss, and a "volcano" with rocks and red substrate.

  4. Place decorations first before adding water.

  5. Try to avoid plastic, painted, dyed, and varnished decorations.

AQUASCAPING

Aquascaping is the art of manipulating the terrain inside an aquarium using plants, rocks, wood, and other decorations. Aquascaping brings out the real potential of fish and plant keeping because it creates beautiful displays of scenery. Aquascaping maintenance takes a lot of work, because you need to be careful not to mess up the delicate plants and design placements. Award-winning aquascapes are usually pieces that depict an above-land scene.
